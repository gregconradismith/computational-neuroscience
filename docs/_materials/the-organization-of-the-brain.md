<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Overview</h2>


<p>By <strong>functional organization of the brain,</strong> we mean an integrated view of neurophysiology, cellular and systems neuroscience, and the anatomy of the central nervous system. The best introductory reading I know of is this classic Scientific American article by Walle_Nauta <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walle_Nauta">Publisher</a> and Michael Feirtag titled "The organization of the brain. <a href="https://www.jstor.org/stable/24965290?seq=1">JSTOR</a>"</p>


<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="../../wp-content/uploads/2025/01/figbrainorganization.png" alt="" class="wp-image-3738" /><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">THREE SENSORIA (hearing, vision, and olfaction) send their information to the cerebral cortex in different ways. (Figure from Nauta &amp; Fertig 1979).</figcaption></figure>


<p>The peripheral sensory apparatus for hearing and vision involves projections to the <strong>thalamus</strong>, but olfaction does not (why?).  Retinal projections to the <strong>lateral geniculate nucleus</strong> of the thalamus, from here to the visual cortex. The retina also projects to the <strong>superior colliculus</strong>.  Auditory projections from the <strong>inferior colliculus</strong> to the <strong>medial geniculate nucleus</strong> of the thalamus, from here to auditory cortex. </p>


<p>As discussed in Butler, A. B., &amp; Hodos, W. (2005),  <strong>lemnothalamic</strong> thalamic nuclei receive sensory projections from the ascending lemniscal systems that do not synapse in the mesencephalic colliculi (inferior and superior colliculus). <strong>Collothalamic</strong> thalamic nuclei receive sensory projections from the mesencephalic colliculi.</p>
