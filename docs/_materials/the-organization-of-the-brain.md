<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Overview</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>By <strong>functional organization of the brain,</strong> we mean an integrated view of neurophysiology, cellular and systems neuroscience, and the anatomy of the central nervous system.  The best introductory reading I know of is this classic Scientific American article by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walle_Nauta">Walle_Nauta</a> and Michael Feirtag titled "<a href="https://www.jstor.org/stable/24965290?seq=1">The organization of the brain.</a>"  </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":3738,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="../../wp-content/uploads/2025/01/figbrainorganization.png" alt="" class="wp-image-3738" /><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">THREE&nbsp;SENSORIA&nbsp;(hearing,&nbsp;vision,&nbsp;and&nbsp;olfaction)&nbsp;send&nbsp;their&nbsp;information&nbsp;to&nbsp;the&nbsp;cerebral&nbsp;cortex&nbsp;in&nbsp;different&nbsp;ways. (Figure from Nauta &amp; Fertig 1979).</figcaption></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The peripheral sensory apparatus for hearing and vision involves projections to the <strong>thalamus</strong>, but olfaction does not (why?).  Retinal projections to the <strong>lateral geniculate nucleus</strong> of the thalamus, from here to the visual cortex. The retina also projects to the&nbsp;<strong>superior colliculus</strong>.  Auditory projections from the&nbsp;<strong>inferior colliculus</strong>&nbsp;to the&nbsp;<strong>medial geniculate nucleus</strong> of the thalamus, from here to auditory cortex. </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>As discussed in Butler, A. B., &amp; Hodos, W. (2005),  <strong>lemnothalamic</strong>&nbsp;thalamic nuclei receive sensory projections from the ascending lemniscal systems that do not synapse in the mesencephalic colliculi (inferior and superior colliculus). <strong>Collothalamic</strong>&nbsp;thalamic nuclei receive sensory projections from the mesencephalic colliculi.</p>
