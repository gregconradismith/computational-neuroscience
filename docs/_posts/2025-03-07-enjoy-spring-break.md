<p>I wish you all a good Spring Break.<br><br>If you want to learn more about computational neuroscience over break, here are two ideas (entirely optional).</p>


<p>You could read <a href="https://apsc450computationalneuroscience.wordpress.com/genealogy-of-the-grandmother-cell/">Genealogy of the “Grandmother Cell</a>” — a short history of neuroscience essay (30 min) that considers philosophical aspects of the concept of <a href="../../pages/feature-detectors-what-the-frogs-eye-tells-the-frogs-brain/">feature detectors</a> in visual processing.  <br><br>As a continuation of our discussion of "sparse coding," you might watch the seminar "<a href="https://youtu.be/6O-EE2JC-yA">From Natural Scene Statistics to Models of Neural Coding &amp; Representation" by Bruno Olshausen</a> (part 1 only, duration 60 min). This talk was part of a summer school on deep learning and feature detection. The target audience is graduate students studying visual neuroscience and/or computer vision.    </p>


<p>Be young.  Be safe.   </p>


<p> --Greg</p>
